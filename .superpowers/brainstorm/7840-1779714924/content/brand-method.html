Brand-build sequence

Stages a brand designer runs before sketching a single logo. Each one answers a question. Each one feeds the next. We'll do them one at a time, in the terminal.

The six stages
  1. Origin — Why does Blab exist? Not the pitch deck reason. The real one. What broke in the world that made you build this?
  2. Audience truth — What does the user actually want, underneath wanting to "learn a language"? (hint: it's rarely the language itself)
  3. Archetype — Pick 1–2 of the 12 universal brand characters (Jester, Hero, Sage, Lover…). Forces a clear character family.
  4. Brand-as-person — Now flesh out the character. Age, job, how they talk in a group, what they wear, what music, what they refuse to do.
  5. Belief + enemy — What does Blab believe? What does it push against? Every strong brand has a villain (Apple vs. PCs, Liquid Death vs. boring water).
  6. Promise — One sentence the user can hold us to. The line that survives every team decision.
Tips before we start
  • Specific beats clever. "For people who hate Duolingo's owl guilt-tripping them" > "For language learners."
  • Brand = trade-off. If it's for everyone, it's for no one. We're going to exclude people on purpose.
  • You already said three things that matter: "honest friend," "can do anything," "comfortable to mess up." Those words will keep showing up — that's the signal we're on track.
  • No wrong answers in stage 1–4. Wrong answers show up in stage 5–6 when nothing connects.
