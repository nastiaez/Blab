Blab Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 June 2026

Blab is a chat app for practising languages with someone you know. This policy explains, in plain language, what information the app handles and who it is shared with. We keep this short on purpose.

The honest version: we don't show ads, we don't sell your data, and we don't track your behaviour for marketing. Your messages are not end-to-end encrypted in this version — that protection is planned for a future update. Today your messages travel over a secure (HTTPS/TLS) connection and are stored on access-controlled servers located in the European Union.

Who is responsible for your data

Blab is operated by [OPERATOR NAME], [city/country] ("we", "us"), who is the data controller for the information described here. You can reach us about privacy at me@aswin.sh. If we don't have an establishment in your region but offer Blab there, we will appoint a local representative where the law requires one.

What we collect

  • Account details — your email address, the display name you choose, and an optional profile photo.
  • Messages — the chats you send and receive, so we can deliver them and show your history.
  • Language preferences — the languages you're learning, to set up your chats and translations.
  • Diagnostics — if the app crashes, we receive a technical error report. These reports are scrubbed so they never contain your message text.

How your messages are translated

To show a translation of a message, the app sends that message's text to a third-party AI translation provider (OpenRouter, which routes to an OpenAI model). The provider returns the translation. The text is sent only to produce the translation; it is not used to show you ads.

Who we share information with

We use a small number of service providers to run the app:

  • Supabase — secure database and hosting for your account and messages (servers in the EU).
  • Google — when you choose "Sign in with Google".
  • OpenRouter / OpenAI — to translate message text, as described above.
  • Sentry — crash and error diagnostics (no message content).

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not share it for advertising. (Under laws like California's CCPA/CPRA, we do not "sell" or "share" your personal information.)

Why we're allowed to use your data (legal basis)

Where laws such as the EU/UK GDPR apply, we process your data to provide the service you asked for (delivering and translating your messages, running your account) and, where relevant, based on your consent (for example an optional profile photo). You can withdraw consent or close your account at any time.

Where your data is processed (international transfers)

Your account and messages are stored on servers in the European Union. Some of our service providers operate outside the EU/your country — for example the translation provider (OpenRouter / OpenAI), Google sign-in, and our crash-diagnostics provider (Sentry) may process data in the United States. Where data leaves your region it is protected by appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses.

Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the EU/UK GDPR, Brazil's LGPD, California's CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws), you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • correct it if it's wrong;
  • delete it (you can delete your account in-app at any time);
  • get a copy of it to take elsewhere;
  • object to or restrict certain processing, and withdraw consent;
  • complain to your local data-protection regulator.

To exercise any of these, email me@aswin.sh. We don't charge for this and we won't treat you differently for asking.

Keeping and deleting your data

We keep your account and messages for as long as your account exists. You can delete your account at any time from Profile → Delete account. Deleting your account removes your profile and your messages from our database. Backups and provider logs are cleared on their normal cycle (typically within 30–90 days) afterwards. We may keep limited records for longer where the law requires it (for example to handle a safety report or legal claim).

How we protect your data

We use HTTPS/TLS encryption in transit, access controls so only the people in a chat can read its messages, and EU-based hosting. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information. If a data breach affects you, we will notify you and the relevant regulators where the law requires.

Cookies and tracking

The Blab app contains no advertising or behaviour-tracking SDKs, and these policy pages set no tracking cookies. We don't build advertising profiles about you.

Automated processing

Translations are produced automatically by an AI service. This is a convenience feature — we don't use it, or any other automated process, to make decisions that have legal or similarly significant effects on you.

Sensitive information

We don't ask for special-category data (such as health, religion, or political views). Because Blab is a chat app, your messages could contain whatever you choose to type — please avoid sharing sensitive personal details you wouldn't want stored.

Information we handle (categories)

For laws that ask us to list categories (such as California's CCPA/CPRA), we handle: identifiers (email, account ID), your name and optional photo, your user content (messages and language settings), and technical diagnostics. We disclose these only to the service providers listed above to run the app, and we do not sell or share them for advertising.

Children

Blab is for people aged 13 and over and is not intended for children under 13. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation; see our Terms of Use for our child-safety standards and how to report concerns.

Changes

If we change this policy we'll update the date at the top. When end-to-end encryption ships, this page will be updated to reflect it.
